Cassine paniculata (Wight & Arn.) Lobr.-Callen - CELASTRACEAE

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Large buttressed tree , up to 40 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , opposite or subopposite ; petiole up to 1.5 cm long, canaliculate ; lamina 7-8 x 2.5-3.5 cm, elliptic , apex acuminate and twisted, base acute to cuneate , margins crenate , glabrous ; midrib raised above; secondary_nerves 7-9 pairs; tertiary_nerves reticulate .
Inflorescence / Flower :Inflorescence cymes ; flower greenish white, petals spathulate .
Fruit and Seed :Drupe , globose , apiculate , 2-seeded .

Ecology :

Emergent trees in the medium elevation evergreen forests between 700 to 1400 m.

Distribution :

Endemic to the Western_Ghats- Anaimalai and Palakkad Hills.

Literatures :

Adansonia ser. 2: 15. 220. 1976; Gamble, Fl. Madras 1: 212. 1997 (re. ed); Sasidharan, Biodiversity documentation for Kerala- Flowering Plants, part 6: 95. 2004.
