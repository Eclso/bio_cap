Bhesa indica (Bedd.) Ding Hou - CELASTRACEAE

Vernacular names :

Tamil: Kadapla. Malayalam: Penali.

Synonyms :

Trochisandra indica Bedd.; Kurrimia bipartite Lawson; Kurrimia indica Gamble

Images :

Languages:

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Large trees , up to 30 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , alternate , spiral , clustered at twig ends, stipules large, to 2.5 cm long, lanceolate , caducous ; petiole 2.5-4.5 cm long, swollen at both ends; lamina 10-18 x 4.5-8.5 cm elliptic-oblong to narrow-ovate , apex obtusely acute or short acuminate with folded, base rounded , coriaceous , shining above, glabrous ; secondary_nerves 11 to 20 pairs, strong, nearly parallel , oblique to midrib ; tertiary_nerves closely_horizontally_percurrent , slender.
Inflorescence / Flower :Inflorescence terminal , panicled racemes ; flowers small, white.
Fruit and Seed :Capsule , red, 2 lobed ; seeds 1 per lobe .

Ecology :

Canopy trees in the medium and high elevation wet evergreen forests from 900-1600 m.

Distribution :

Indomalaysia; in the Western_Ghats- South Sahyadri.

Literatures :

Blumea suppl. 4. 152. 1958; Gamble, Fl. Madras 1: 207. 1997 (re. ed); Sasidharan, Biodiversity documentation for Kerala- Flowering Plants, part 6: 95. 2004.
