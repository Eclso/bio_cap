Oroxylum indicum (L.) Benth. ex Kurz - BIGNONIACEAE

Vernacular names Images Botanical descriptions Ecology Distribution Literatures

Vernacular names :

Tamil: Achi pana, Arandei, Paiyaralandai, Palaiyudaycci, Peiarlankei, Palagaipayani, Vangam. Malayalam: Palakapayyani, Payyazhantha, Valpathiri, Aralu. Kannada: Alangi, Bunepaale, Patagani, Sonepatta, Tattuna, Tigadu. English: Indian trumpet flower.

Synonyms :

Bignonia indica L.

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Small trees , up to 12 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves compound , 2-3_pinnate , with opposite pinnae , very large 90-150 cm long, almost triangular in outline; primary rachis stout, cylindric; secondary and tertiary rachis striate ; petiolule 5.5 cm long, striate ; leaflets 2-4 pairs with terminal odd one, opposite , lamina 7-17 x 3.5-10 cm, ovate or elliptic , apex caudate - acuminate , base rounded or asymmetric , margin entire , chartaceous , glabrous ; midrib flat above; 1-2 pairs of secondary_nerves arising from base; tertiary_nerves broadly reticulate .
Inflorescence / Flower :Flowers in terminal racemes , large, reddish-purple and pinkish-yellow within.
Fruit and Seed :Capsule 30-90 cm long, flat, purple brown; seeds many, flattened with broad hyaline papery wings .

Ecology :

Along margins of evergreen and in moist deciduous forests, up to 800 m.

Distribution :

Indochina and Indomalaysia; in the Western_Ghats- occasional from South to Central and south Maharashtra Sahyadris and West Coast Region.
