Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Large shrubs or small trees up to 5 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , alternate , spiral , clustered at twig ends; petiole ca. 0.2 cm long, stout, swollen at base, planoconvex in cross section; lamina 2.5-6.5 x 0.5-1.2 cm, narrow elliptic to oblanceolate , apex acuminate to acute with apiculate tip, base decurrent , margin entire and revolute , shining above, glabrous , viscid ; midrib raised above; secondary nerves ca. 16 pairs, nearly parallel ; tertiary nerves closely reticulate .

Ecology :

Common in dry scrub as shrubs ; however, also found along margin of high elevation evergreen forests as trees , up to 2400 m.

Pantropical ; in the Western_Ghats- throughout.
