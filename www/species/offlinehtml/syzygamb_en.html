Syzygium gambleanum Rathakr. & Chitra - MYRTACEAE

Vernacular names :

Malayalam: Cherunjaval

Synonyms :

Eugenia microphylla Bedd.; Syzygium microphyllum Gamble

Images :

Languages:

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Trees up to 10 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , opposite , decussate ; petiole 0.1 cm long, canaliculate , glabrous ; lamina small, 0.6-3 x 0.2-1 cm, lanceolate , elliptic to obovate , apex obtuse , base acute - rounded , margin revolute , pellucid gland dotted, coriaceous , glabrous ; midrib canaliculate ; secondary_nerves and tertiary_nerves obscure .
Inflorescence / Flower :Inflorescence terminal and axillary umbels , rarely solitary ; flowers white; pedicel 0.2 cm long.
Fruit and Seed :Berry 0.8×5 cm, globose to oblong , crowned by persistent calyx , purple; seed one.

Ecology :

Along the margin of exposed evergreen forests between 1100 and 1400 m.

Distribution :

Endemic to the southern Western_Ghats- Agasthyamalai hills.

Literatures :

Henry & Nair, Fl. Tamil Nadu Ser. 1, 1: 156. 1983; Gamble, Fl. Madras 1: 479.1997 (re.ed); Sasidharan, Biodiversity documentation for Kerala- Flowering Plants, part 6: 176. 2004; Sheeba et al., 2003. Bull. Bot. Surv. India 45: 111.
