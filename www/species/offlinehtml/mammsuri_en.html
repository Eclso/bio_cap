Mammea suriga (Buch.-Ham. ex Roxb.) Kosterm. - CLUSIACEAE

Vernacular names Images Botanical descriptions Ecology Distribution Literatures

Vernacular names :

Malayalam: Seraya, Suran-punna Kannada: Wundi, Punay, Suringi

Synonyms :

Calophyllum suriga Buch.-Ham. ex Roxb.; Ochrocarpus longifolius (Wt.) Anders.

Images :

Languages:

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Trees up to 15 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , opposite , decussate ; petiole 0.5-1.0 cm long, canaliculate , glabrous ; lamina 10-23 x 4-7.5 cm, oblong , narrow-oblong to narrow obovate , apex obtuse or bluntly short acuminate , base obtuse , thickly coriaceous ; midrib indistinct at apex ; secondary_nerves many, parallel , perpendicular to midrib ; tertiary_nerves finely reticulate with tiny transparent glands at the centre of areoles , sometimes invisible due to thick cuticle.
Inflorescence / Flower :Flowers polygamous , white, clustered on old branches with elongated pedicels .
Fruit and Seed :Berry , elliptic , beaked ; 1-seeded .

Ecology :

In open evergreen forests up to 700 m.

Distribution :

Endemic to the Western_Ghats- from North Malabar Coast to South Konkan Coast and in northern part of Central Sahyadri.

Literatures :

Comm. For. Res. Inst. Indones. Bogor 72: 33. 1961; Gamble, Fl. Madras 1: 75. 1997 (re. ed); Sasidharan, Biodiversity documentation for Kerala- Flowering Plants, part 6: 42. 2004; Saldanha, Fl. Karnataka 1: 209. 1996; Cook, Fl. Bombay 1: 79. 1902.
