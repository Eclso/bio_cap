Linociera courtallensis Bedd.; Linociera leprocarpa var. courtallensis (Bedd.) Clarke; Chionanthus leprocarpa Thw. var. courtallensis Nair Janardh.

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Trees up to 22 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , opposite , decussate ; petiole 0.5-0.8 cm long, canaliculate , glabrous ; lamina 9-15.5 x 3-5 cm, oblong to elliptic-oblong , apex acuminate or caudate - acuminate , base acute or cuneate , margin entire , glabrous beneath, coriaceous , reddish brown when dry; midrib nearly flat above; secondary_nerves 6-10 pairs, slender; tertiary_nerves broadly reticulate and impressed beneath or obscure .
Inflorescence / Flower :Inflorescence axillary racemes ; flowers white.
Fruit and Seed :Drupe ; seed one.

Ecology :

Subcanopy trees in wet evergreen forests between 700 and 1700 m.

Distribution :

Endemic to the Western_Ghats- Agasthyamalai hills and Elamalai in South Sahyadri.
