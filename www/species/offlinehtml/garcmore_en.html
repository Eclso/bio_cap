Garcinia morella (Gaertn.) Desr. - CLUSIACEAE

Vernacular names Images Botanical descriptions Ecology Distribution Literatures

Vernacular names :

Tamil: Iravasinni, Makki. Malayalam: Chigiri, Iravi. Kannada: Ardala, Devana huli, Jirigehuli, Murina huli, Ponpuli. English: Sri Lanka gamboge, Indian gamboge (T).

Synonyms :

Mangostana morella Gaertn.; Garcinia elliptica Wall.; G. gutta Wt.

Languages:

Botanical descriptions :

Habit :Trees up to 12 m tall.
Leaves :Leaves simple , opposite , decussate ; petiole 0.6-1.5 cm long, canaliculate , sheathing at base, glabrous ; lamina 6.5-15 x 3.5-8 cm, usually elliptic , sometimes narrow obovate , apex acute to acuminate , base attenuate ; coriaceous or subcoriaceous , glabrous ; secondary_nerves 6-8 pairs; tertiary_nerves obscure .
Inflorescence / Flower :Flowers dioecious ; male flowers in fascicles , axillary ; female flowers larger than male , solitary , axillary .
Fruit and Seed :Berry , 3 cm across, 4-seeded .

Ecology :

Common understorey trees in evergreen forests up to 1200 m.

Distribution :

Indomalaysia; in the Western_Ghats- through out South and Central Sahyadris.

Literatures :

Lamarck, Encycl. 3: 701. 405. 1792; Gamble, Fl. Madras 1: 73. 1997 (re. ed); Sasidharan, Biodiversity documentation for Kerala- Flowering Plants, part 6: 41. 2004; Cook, Fl. Bombay 1: 77. 1902.
